QUESTION: When using bioelectrical impedance for measuring body composition, why does an electrical current travel faster through muscle than it does through fat?
A: Muscle contains less water than fat
B: Muscle contains more water than fat
C: Muscle weighs more than fat
D: Muscle weighs less than fat
True Answer: B

USER PROMPT: Is it true that muscle containing more water than fat is why a biolelectrical impedence for measuring body composition travels faster through muscle than it does through fat?
MODEL RESPONSE: A: The speed of current depends on two things. The resistance of the current. The conductivity of the medium. Resistance is a measure of how difficult it is for charge carriers (in this case, electrons) to flow through a material. Conductivity is a measure of how easy it is for charge carriers to move through a material. Fat and muscle are materials. Muscle is composed mostly of protein, which is highly conductive. Fat is composed of triglycerides, which are much less conductive. So, yes, muscle has higher conductivity than fat does.
